A Review on the first year of married life of aspiring PhD couple

https://doi.org/10.1016/j.matpr.2023.02.036Get rights and content

Abstract

The present study attempts to review the significance of important incidents, the feelings induced and the outpour of emotions in the first year of married life of the authors. The paper studies in detail about the incidents leading up to the marriage and the events that unfolded in the months following the marriage. The incidents have been reviewed along with pertinent images to induce a similar feeling when reading about the incident and to give a small taste of numerous cherishable memories that the authors have built together. The review of the events from the "memory journal" lead to the conclusion that the authors love each other like crazy and they chose to work on their marriage in spite of the numerous verbal battles they had faced. The study also found that there is a high possibility of the author living together for another fifty years in happiness, cherishing their memories together.

Introduction

The aerospace, aviation and the automotive industry leaders are striving towards an eco-friendlier approach in the manufacturing and the utilization of their products. One of the major focal points being addressed in order to achieve this outcome is the reduction of the total mass of the products by increasing the usage of light-weight but stronger materials [1], [2]. The materials that are being considered as the frontiers in attaining this goal are aluminum and magnesium alloys to take the place of traditional steel owing to the high specific strength displayed by both the materials. Copious number of studies have been conducted on the effect of repetitive alternating loads on the Nf of bolted joints, as they are usually the weakest links in a mechanism [3], [4], [5], [6], [7]. Thus, the study of the behavior of these materials under repetitive loading is paramount as they will often be loaded cyclically.

Several researchers have published their works on the fatigue strength of magnesium alloys loaded uniaxially [8], [9], [10], [11], [12], and a few novel methodologies have also been recommended to determine the fatigue strength of magnesium alloy. Liu et al. [13] suggested the usage of thermal indicators for investigating AZ31B, where an infrared thermography method was utilized to study the temperature distribution under repetitive alternative loading to appraise the fatigue strength. It was noted that the Nf calculated by Smith-Watson-Topper criterion (SWTC) had good agreement with the experimental results. The studies conducted by Babak Abazadeh et al. [14] presented a comprehensive comparison of seven multi-axial fatigue criteria and it was concluded that SWTC had the best results for estimating the life of bolted joints made of 2024-aluminium alloy. The research work by Esmaeli et al demonstrated an accurate process for appraising the Nf of the bolted joints using finite element method (FEM) [15].

The current work aims to study and analyze the effects of the different materials and the fastening forces on the Nf of the bolt joint specimen using numerical analysis. To achieve this objective, the fatigue lives of the standard bolted joints made of 2024-T3 aluminum alloy and AZ31-B magnesium alloy were assessed at four different fastening forces imparted by the tightening of the bolt. The distribution of stresses in the critical region adjacent to the bolt holes were obtained by implementing finite element analysis. ANSYS was implemented to accomplish this objective [16], [17]. The fatigue lives of these were assessed from the above using SWTC, owing to its accuracy as inferred from the literature.
